Roots in the 1960s

Our roots go back to the 1960s when my Dad purchased and operated Severance Garage. His focus was mechanic work, then later introduced paint and body work.

He has always been a car enthusiast since the 50s — when he purchased his first new car, a 1957 Chevy 2-door hardtop, black with red interior. His passion was transferred to me as I grew up around the business.

“A strong desire to build muscle cars developed — and it never left.”

Early experiences with Hot Wheels and plastic model builds fueled my desire for the real thing. A 1971 Rally Sport Z/28 Camaro was the first build I was involved with in 1980. My first personal car — a 1968 Firebird 400 — was restored in 1981.

My Autobody business began in 1989. Since 2005, my entire focus has been high-end rotisserie restorations. First generation Camaro builds are my specialty with over 100 completed.
